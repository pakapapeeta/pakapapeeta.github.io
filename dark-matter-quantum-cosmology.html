Dark Matter & Quantum Cosmology Research

Probing the fundamental nature of dark matter through quantum cosmological models and observational constraints

Research Overview

My research investigates the intricate relationship between dark matter and quantum effects in the early universe. By combining cutting-edge observational data from surveys like DESI with theoretical frameworks from quantum gravity, I explore how quantum corrections influence dark matter properties and cosmic structure formation.

Key Research Objectives

  • Non-Cold Dark Matter Models: Investigating warm and fuzzy dark matter scenarios that deviate from standard CDM predictions
  • Quantum Gravity Effects: Studying quantum corrections to cosmological evolution and their observational signatures
  • Structure Formation: Analyzing how alternative dark matter models affect galaxy formation and large-scale structure
  • Observational Constraints: Using DESI, Planck, and other survey data to constrain theoretical models

Research Methodology

  • Theoretical Modeling: Developing quantum field theory models for dark matter interactions
  • Numerical Simulations: Running N-body simulations with modified dark matter physics
  • Statistical Analysis: Bayesian parameter estimation and model comparison techniques
  • Multi-wavelength Data: Cross-correlating optical, X-ray, and gravitational lensing observations
