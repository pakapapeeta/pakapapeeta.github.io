Research Overview

My research focuses on understanding the fundamental nature of dark matter and its role in shaping the large-scale structure of the universe. By combining observational data from surveys like DESI with theoretical models from quantum gravity, I investigate how quantum effects in the early universe could influence dark matter properties.

Key Research Areas

  • Non-Cold Dark Matter Models: Investigating warm and fuzzy dark matter scenarios
  • Quantum Gravity Effects: Studying quantum corrections to cosmological evolution
  • Structure Formation: Analyzing how alternative dark matter affects galaxy formation
  • Observational Constraints: Using DESI and other survey data to constrain models

Gravitational Wave Background Research

Probing early universe physics through stochastic gravitational wave signals

NANOGrav & Early Universe Cosmology

Using pulsar timing array data from NANOGrav to constrain early universe models and search for primordial gravitational wave backgrounds. This work bridges observational astronomy with fundamental physics, offering new windows into cosmic inflation and alternative cosmological scenarios.

Research Methodology

  • Data Analysis: NANOGrav 15-year data set analysis
  • Theoretical Modeling: Bouncing cosmology and string theory predictions
  • Statistical Methods: Bayesian parameter estimation
  • Cross-correlation: Multi-frequency gravitational wave analysis
